## Executive Summary

The rules of digital visibility have fundamentally changed. AI-powered search is now a dominant discovery channel, with ChatGPT processing 3B+ prompts monthly, Perplexity indexing 200B+ URLs, and Google AI Overviews appearing on ~16% of searches. The question is no longer "how do I rank?" but **"how do I get cited by AI?"**

### The 5 Most Important Findings

1. **Brand search volume (not backlinks) is the #1 predictor of LLM citations** ‚Äî correlation coefficient of 0.334, the strongest measured factor (Source: Digital Bloom 7,000-citation analysis)
2. **The Princeton GEO study proves optimisation works** ‚Äî specific content strategies can boost AI visibility by up to 40% (Source: KDD 2024 paper, arXiv:2311.09735)
3. **Only 11% of domains get cited by BOTH ChatGPT and Perplexity** ‚Äî each platform has fundamentally different citation patterns, requiring platform-specific strategies (Source: Digital Bloom 2025 Report)
4. **Wikipedia dominates training data (~22%) and citations (~43-48% of ChatGPT conversations)** ‚Äî you can't beat Wikipedia, but you can be the next source after it (Source: Profound 700K conversation analysis)
5. **llms.txt has zero measurable impact** ‚Äî a Senthor study of 10M+ AI requests found 0 major bots reading it; SE Ranking's 300K-domain study confirmed no correlation with citations (Sources: Senthor, SE Ranking)

## Section 1: How Each AI Model Sources Information

### 1.1 ChatGPT (OpenAI)

**Architecture:** Hybrid system ‚Äî parametric knowledge (training data) + real-time web retrieval via Bing

**Evidence Strength:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Multiple large-scale studies with 700K+ conversations analysed)

#### Two Modes of Operation

1. **Base Knowledge Mode (Parametric):** Draws from training corpus. ~60% of queries answered purely from parametric knowledge without triggering web search. No citations provided in this mode.
   - Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 AI Citation Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

2. **Web Search Mode (RAG):** Searches the web in real-time using Bing. Activates automatically for queries requiring current information. Displays inline citations with clickable source links.
   - Source: [OptimizeYour.Blog ‚Äî How ChatGPT Finds and Cites Content](https://optimizeyour.blog/learn/platforms/how-chatgpt-finds-and-cites-content)

#### Training Data Hierarchy (Parametric Knowledge)

OpenAI's training data is prioritised in tiers:
- **Tier 1:** Wikipedia, licensed publisher partners (Cond√© Nast, Vox Media), GPTBot-accessible sites
- **Tier 2:** Reddit content with 3+ upvotes, industry publications
- **Tier 3:** YouTube transcripts, podcasts

Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

#### Web Search Source Selection (When Browsing is Enabled)

- ChatGPT queries Bing and selects 3-10 diverse sources per response
- **87% of SearchGPT citations match Bing's top 10 organic results** (Seer Interactive analysis of 500+ citations)
- Only 56% correlation with Google results
- 3.2x more often ChatGPT **mentions** brands than it actually **cites** them with links
- Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

#### Citation Patterns (from Profound 700K Conversation Analysis)

- **~18% of ChatGPT conversations trigger at least one web search**
- **Turn 1 is everything:** 2.5x more likely to trigger citations than Turn 10; 4x more than Turn 20
- **~6 unique citations per conversation** (trending upward Oct-Dec 2025)
- **~4 unique citations per turn** when a turn includes any citations
- **Wikipedia appears in ~1 in 6 conversations** with citations (~5% of all citations, ~18% of conversations with citations)
- **Reddit appears in ~13% of conversations** with citations
- **Top 10 domains capture only 12% of all citations** but distribution is highly unequal (Gini = 0.8)

Source: [Profound ‚Äî How ChatGPT Sources the Web](https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/chatgpt-citation-sources) (Feb 2026, 700K+ conversations, Q4 2025)

#### Citation by Query Type

| Query Type | Top Source | % | Second Source | % |
|-----------|-----------|---|---------------|---|
| General Information | Wikipedia | ~43% | Reddit | ~12% |
| Commerce | Wikipedia | ~22% | Amazon | ~19% |
| Local Business | Direct business websites | ~58% | Wikipedia + news | ~27% |

Source: [OptimizeYour.Blog](https://optimizeyour.blog/learn/platforms/how-chatgpt-finds-and-cites-content)

#### Domain Category Breakdown (Wellows 485K Citation Analysis)

| Domain Category | Citation Share |
|----------------|---------------|
| Tech Media & Publishers | ~46.5% |
| Product & SaaS Websites | ~25.3% |
| Other (niche, community) | ~16.8% |
| Education & Research | ~6.6% |
| Consulting & Analyst | ~4.8% |

**Key insight:** Top 50 domains = 48% of citations, but the remaining 52% goes to 38,000+ long-tail domains. **Niche expertise can compete with authority.**

Source: [Wellows ‚Äî Cited by ChatGPT: 7K Queries, 485K Citations](https://wellows.com/insights/chatgpt-citations-report/) (Jul 2025)

#### Media Citation Share: 15.4%
ChatGPT shows the most balanced and distributed citation pattern of all platforms, spreading media authority across 82 distinct outlets with no single publication exceeding 2.1% share.

Source: [Britopian ‚Äî Generative Search Citations](https://www.britopian.com/research/generative-search-citations-tech-media-outlets/) (Nov 2025)

#### Publisher Partnerships
OpenAI has licensing deals with: News Corp, The Washington Post, Associated Press, Reuters, Financial Times, The Atlantic, Time, and Vox Media ‚Äî these get preferential access.

Source: [OptimizeYour.Blog](https://optimizeyour.blog/learn/platforms/how-chatgpt-finds-and-cites-content)

### 1.2 Claude (Anthropic)

**Architecture:** Parametric knowledge + Brave Search integration for web retrieval

**Evidence Strength:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Less independent research available compared to ChatGPT)

#### Knowledge Retrieval

- Claude's knowledge retrieval is shaped by Anthropic's **Constitutional AI framework**, creating strong preferences for **helpful, harmless, and honest** content
- When using web search (powered by **Brave Search**), Claude autonomously determines search necessity and provides citations with URL, title, and cited_text snippets
- Claude **cross-references at least three external sources** before surfacing a claim, prioritising accuracy over recency
- Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Snezzi ‚Äî How AI Chatbots Pick Sources](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

#### Source Selection Behaviour

- **Conservative approach:** May favour older, well-verified information over newer, less-confirmed data
- **Methodology-focused:** Research papers with clear methodology sections, articles citing primary sources, and content demonstrating rigorous fact-checking receive preferential treatment
- **Strict on controversial topics:** Requires additional verification steps for high-misinformation-risk subjects
- Source: [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

#### What's Uncertain About Claude
- Exact training data composition is not publicly documented
- How Claude weights brand mentions vs. domain authority vs. content quality is not studied at scale
- **No large-scale citation analysis exists for Claude specifically** (unlike ChatGPT and Perplexity)

### 1.3 Perplexity AI

**Architecture:** Real-time RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) ‚Äî every query triggers real-time web search

**Evidence Strength:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Indexed architecture is well-documented; multiple studies)

#### How It Works

- Every query triggers real-time web search against a **proprietary index of 200+ billion URLs**
- Processed at tens of thousands of indexing operations per second across 400+ petabytes of storage
- Provides **5-10 inline citations per response** with numbered references
- **Real-time index refreshes** in near real-time ‚Äî new content can appear within hours
- Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

#### Source Selection

| Source Type | % of Top 10 Citations |
|------------|----------------------|
| Reddit | 46.7% |
| YouTube | 13.9% |
| Gartner | 7.0% |

**Key difference from ChatGPT:** Perplexity favours **structured buying guides and comparison content** over traditional news. Reddit dominates because Perplexity heavily weights community-validated information.

Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

#### Media Citation Share: 8.8%
Only cites 45 media outlets total (vs ChatGPT's 82). PCMag and Forbes dominate. Strong preference for systematic product evaluations over news.

Source: [Britopian](https://www.britopian.com/research/generative-search-citations-tech-media-outlets/)

### 1.4 Google AI Overviews / Gemini

**Architecture:** Gemini LLMs + Google's existing search index and ranking signals

**Evidence Strength:** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê (Semrush study of 10M+ keywords; Pew Research Center data)

#### How AI Overviews Work

- Generated using Google's Gemini models by gathering information from multiple web sources
- Uses **query fan-out**: running multiple related searches to compile comprehensive responses
- Evaluates content quality, source authority, and relevance as primary signals
- **93.67% of AI Overview citations link to at least one top-10 organic result**
- But only **4.5% of AI Overview URLs directly matched a Page 1 organic URL** ‚Äî Google draws from deeper pages on authoritative domains
- Average: **10.2 links from 4 unique domains per AI Overview response**
- Source: [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Semrush AI Overviews Study](https://www.semrush.com/blog/semrush-ai-overviews-study/)

#### AI Overview Prevalence

- Started at 6.49% of queries (Jan 2025), peaked at 24.61% (Jul 2025), settled at 15.69% (Nov 2025)
- 50%+ of searches show AI Overviews (up from 18% in March 2025) in some contexts
- Expanding beyond informational: Commercial queries up from 8% to 19%; Transactional up from 2% to 14%
- Source: [Semrush AI Overviews Study](https://www.semrush.com/blog/semrush-ai-overviews-study/)

#### Click Behaviour Impact

- Users click traditional search result links **8% of the time** when AI Overview present (vs 15% without)
- Users click results **within** the AI Overview only **1% of the time**
- Users more likely to **end browsing session entirely** (26% vs 16%)
- Most frequently cited sources in AI summaries: Wikipedia, YouTube, Reddit (15% collectively)
- Government websites (.gov) more common in AI summaries (6%) than standard results (2%)
- Source: [Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/) (Jul 2025)

#### Media Citation Share: 7.6% (AI Overviews) / 5.7% (Gemini)
- AI Overviews: Most diversified citation base (96 outlets) but each gets small share. Tolerates older, evergreen content.
- Gemini: Lowest media citation share (5.7%). Only 35 media outlets cited. Forbes alone = 23% of all Gemini media citations. Strongly favours direct sources, product documentation, and structured data.
- Source: [Britopian](https://www.britopian.com/research/generative-search-citations-tech-media-outlets/)

#### Google's Official Position
"Apply the same foundational SEO best practices for AI features as you do for Google Search overall."
- Source: [Google Developers Documentation](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-overviews)

## Section 2: Proven Citation/Recommendation Factors

### 2.1 Brand Search Volume
**Impact: üî¥ CRITICAL (Strongest measured factor)**

- **Correlation coefficient of 0.334 with AI visibility** ‚Äî the strongest predictor found in any study
- Sites mentioned on **4+ platforms are 2.8x more likely** to appear in ChatGPT responses
- This means: if people search for your brand by name on Google, you're more likely to be cited by AI

**Evidence strength:** Proven by study (7,000+ citations across 1,600 URLs)
**Platforms affected:** All
**Source:** [Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

### 2.2 Content Quality & Citation Methodology (GEO Strategies)
**Impact: üî¥ CRITICAL**

The Princeton GEO study (10,000 queries, 9 sources) proved specific optimisation methods work:

| Method | Visibility Impact | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| **Cite Sources Method** | **+115.1%** (for rank #5 sites) | Adding references and citations to your content |
| **Quotation Addition** | **+37%** | Adding expert quotes (especially effective on Perplexity) |
| **Statistics Addition** | **+22%** | Including specific numbers and data points |
| **Fluency Optimisation** | **+15-30%** | Improving readability and flow |
| **Keyword Stuffing** | **NEGATIVE impact** | Actively harmful |

**Critical insight:** Lower-ranked traditional SERP sites benefit *significantly more* from GEO optimisation than top-ranked sites ‚Äî making this a powerful challenger strategy.

**Evidence strength:** Proven by peer-reviewed academic study (KDD 2024)
**Platforms affected:** All (tested across multiple generative engines)
**Source:** [Princeton GEO Paper ‚Äî arXiv:2311.09735](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)

### 2.3 Content Structure & Format
**Impact: üî¥ CRITICAL**

#### High-Citation Content Formats (from 30M+ citations):

**Answer-first structures and scannable subheadings increase selection odds by up to 39%**

**Evidence strength:** Proven by multiple industry studies
**Platforms affected:** All

### 2.4 Structured Data (Schema.org / JSON-LD)
**Impact: üü† HIGH**

A Search Engine Land experiment tested three identical single-page sites differing only in schema quality:

| Schema Quality | Google Rank | AI Overview |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Well-implemented | Position 3 | YES ‚Äî Appeared |
| Poorly implemented | Position 8 | No appearance |
| No schema | NOT INDEXED | N/A |

- **47% higher AI citation rates** for comparison tables using proper `<thead>` and descriptive columns
- Sites with clear schema markup and fast mobile performance see **47% higher likelihood of being cited** by AI chatbots
- Wikidata is the #1 source for Google's Knowledge Graph (500B facts, 5B entities)
- **sameAs property linking to Wikidata increases entity recognition**

**Priority Schema Types:**
- Tier 1 (Essential): HowTo, Article/BlogPosting, Organization, Person
- Tier 2 (High Value): Product/Offer, LocalBusiness, Review/AggregateRating, Speakable

**Evidence strength:** Proven by controlled experiment (Search Engine Land); corroborated by industry data
**Platforms affected:** Primarily Google AI Overviews and Gemini; moderate effect on ChatGPT/Perplexity
**Sources:** [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

### 2.5 E-E-A-T Signals (Expertise, Experience, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
**Impact: üü† HIGH**

AI models assess E-E-A-T through:
- **Author credentials and institutional affiliations** ‚Äî content by recognised experts gets preferential treatment
- **Transparent methodology** ‚Äî explaining how products were tested or research was conducted
- **Citation chains** ‚Äî sites that cite their own sources perform better
- **Publication dates and update timestamps** ‚Äî freshness signals
- **Claude specifically:** Constitutional AI framework creates strong preferences for helpful, harmless, honest content

**Evidence strength:** Industry consensus, supported by multiple observational studies
**Platforms affected:** All (especially Claude and Google)
**Sources:** [Outpace SEO](https://outpaceseo.com/article/how-generative-ai-cites-sources/); [Skyscale](https://skyscale.com.au/blogs/how-chatgpt-selects-sources); [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

### 2.6 Domain Authority
**Impact: üü° MODERATE (but nuanced)**

- **73% of enterprise AI deployments reference domain authority metrics** when ranking external information sources
- **61% of chatbot-attributed links go to sites with 100+ referring domains**
- **Light preference for ChatGPT** in particular; weaker signal for Perplexity
- However, the Princeton GEO study found that **traditional backlinks show weak or neutral correlation** with AI citations ‚Äî contradicting traditional SEO wisdom

**The nuance:** Domain authority matters as a baseline, but content quality and brand recognition matter more. A high-DA site with poor content will lose to a low-DA site with excellent, well-structured content.

**Evidence strength:** Mixed ‚Äî some studies show correlation; others show it's less important than expected
**Platforms affected:** Moderate across all; strongest for Google AI Overviews
**Sources:** [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

### 2.7 Content Freshness
**Impact: üü† HIGH**

- **65% of AI bot hits target content published within the past year**
- **79% from content updated within 2 years**
- **Only 6% from content older than 6 years**
- Pages updated within **6-18 months** are weighted most highly
- For trending topics, ChatGPT may apply strict recency filters (past week or days only)
- However, authority wins for evergreen topics ‚Äî a definitive 2023 guide can outrank a thin 2025 post

**Evidence strength:** Proven by study (300,000+ keywords and 5,000+ URLs)
**Platforms affected:** All (especially Perplexity due to real-time indexing)
**Sources:** [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Wellows](https://wellows.com/insights/chatgpt-citations-report/)

### 2.8 Topical Authority / Comprehensive Coverage
**Impact: üü† HIGH**

- AI systems pay attention to websites that present **expert information about specific areas** rather than mentioning topics briefly
- Comprehensive guides earn more citations than short posts
- **Topic clusters** (pillar page + supporting articles) help AI understand content relationships
- An article with **10,000+ words and Flesch Score 55 received 187 total citations** (72 from ChatGPT), while similar content under 4,000 words with lower readability received only 3 citations

**Evidence strength:** Proven by study (Digital Bloom citation analysis)
**Platforms affected:** All
**Sources:** [Outpace SEO](https://outpaceseo.com/article/how-generative-ai-cites-sources/); [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

### 2.9 Direct Answers / Answer-First Structure
**Impact: üü† HIGH**

- Lead with the answer: "The best X is Y" not "Y might be good"
- Answer the question immediately, then provide supporting details
- FAQ sections that address common related questions are particularly effective
- **39% increase in selection odds** with answer-first structures and scannable subheadings
- ChatGPT rewrites queries into targeted search terms ‚Äî pages matching these rewritten queries get selected

**Evidence strength:** Industry consensus, supported by the Princeton GEO study
**Platforms affected:** All
**Sources:** [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/); [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)

### 2.10 Wikipedia Presence
**Impact: üü° MODERATE-HIGH**

- Wikipedia content represents **~22% of major LLM training data**
- Wikipedia is the #1 cited source by ChatGPT (~43-48% of conversations with citations)
- Most frequently cited source in both Google AI summaries AND standard search results
- **Having a Wikipedia page** likely increases your presence in training data and thus parametric knowledge
- **Wikidata** serves as the #1 source for Google's Knowledge Graph (500B facts, 5B entities)

**Evidence strength:** Strong statistical evidence from multiple studies; mechanism well-understood
**Platforms affected:** All (especially ChatGPT and Google)
**Sources:** [Profound](https://www.tryprofound.com/blog/chatgpt-citation-sources); [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/07/22/google-users-are-less-likely-to-click-on-links-when-an-ai-summary-appears-in-the-results/)

### 2.11 Reddit Mentions
**Impact: üü° MODERATE-HIGH**

### 2.13 llms.txt
**Impact: ‚ö™ NONE (Currently)**

**This is one of the most over-hyped "factors" in AI SEO. The evidence is clear: it doesn't work.**

- **Senthor study: 10M+ certified AI requests, 0 reads by major bots.** 104 total requests came from curious humans and small unidentified scrapers ‚Äî not OpenAI, Google, or Anthropic.
- **SE Ranking study: 300,000 domains analysed.** No correlation between llms.txt presence and AI citation frequency. Removing llms.txt from their XGBoost model actually **improved** accuracy ‚Äî it was adding noise.
- **OtterlyAI 90-day experiment:** Zero measurable effect on AI visibility.
- Only ~10% of websites have an llms.txt file
- No major AI platform has officially adopted llms.txt as a standard
- Google explicitly states AI Overviews rely on traditional SEO signals, not llms.txt

**Evidence strength:** Definitively disproven by multiple independent studies
**Platforms affected:** None
**Sources:** [Senthor ‚Äî 10M AI Requests Study](https://www.senthor.io/en/blog/data-study-llms-txt-vs-reality) (Jan 2026); [SE Ranking ‚Äî 300K Domains Study](https://seranking.com/blog/llms-txt/) (Nov 2025)

### 2.14 Backlink Profile
**Impact: üü° MODERATE (Weaker than expected)**

- Digital Bloom's analysis found backlinks show **"weak or neutral" correlation** with AI citations ‚Äî contradicting traditional SEO
- 61% of chatbot-attributed links go to sites with 100+ referring domains (but this may be correlation, not causation ‚Äî authoritative sites naturally have more backlinks)
- **Backlinks matter more for Google AI Overviews** (which strongly correlate with organic rankings) than for ChatGPT or Perplexity
- **Brand mentions may matter more than backlinks** for AI visibility specifically

**Evidence strength:** Mixed signals ‚Äî traditional correlation exists but weaker than expected
**Platforms affected:** Primarily Google AI Overviews; weak for ChatGPT/Perplexity
**Sources:** [Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/); [Snezzi](https://snezzi.com/blog/how-ai-chatbots-pick-sources-an-inside-look-for-2025-marketers/)

### 2.15 Page Speed / Technical SEO
**Impact: üü¢ LOW-MODERATE**

### 3.1 The Princeton GEO Paper (Seminal Work)

**Title:** "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization"
**Authors:** Pranjal Aggarwal et al. (Princeton University, Georgia Tech, Allen Institute for AI, IIT Delhi)
**Published:** KDD 2024 (30th ACM SIGKDD Conference), Barcelona, Spain
**Citations:** 59 (as of Feb 2026)
**ArXiv:** [2311.09735](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)
**DOI:** [10.1145/3637528.3671900](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3637528.3671900)

#### Key Findings

1. **GEO optimisation methods can boost visibility by up to 40%** in generative engine responses
2. **Efficacy varies by domain** ‚Äî no one-size-fits-all; domain-specific optimisation is needed
3. Introduces **GEO-bench**, a large-scale benchmark of diverse user queries across multiple domains
4. **Lower-ranked sites benefit more** from GEO than already top-ranked sites
5. Specific strategies tested:
   - Adding citations to content: +115.1% visibility for rank #5 sites
   - Adding expert quotations: +37% improvement
   - Adding statistics: +22% improvement
   - Fluency improvements: +15-30% boost
   - Keyword stuffing: **Negative** impact (-9% or worse)

**Significance:** This is the foundational academic work in the field. It proves that content can be systematically optimised for AI engines, establishes measurement frameworks, and provides the first rigorous evidence of which strategies work.

## Section 4: Platform-Specific Optimisation Playbooks

### 4.1 How to Get Cited by ChatGPT

**Confidence level:** HIGH ‚Äî extensive research data available

#### Must-Do Actions

1. **Ensure Bing indexation** ‚Äî 87% of ChatGPT web citations come from Bing's top 10. If you're not indexed in Bing, you're invisible to ChatGPT Search.
   - Submit your sitemap to [Bing Webmaster Tools](https://www.bing.com/webmasters/)
   - Implement IndexNow for instant indexing

2. **Build brand search volume** ‚Äî The #1 predictor (0.334 correlation). Invest in brand awareness through PR, content marketing, and cross-platform presence.

3. **Get mentioned on 4+ platforms** ‚Äî Sites on 4+ platforms are 2.8x more likely to appear. Ensure presence on your website + Wikipedia/Wikidata + Reddit + industry publications + review sites.

4. **Win the "first question"** ‚Äî Turn 1 conversations capture most citations. Optimise for queries that START research journeys: "what is X", "how does Y work", "best Z for [use case]".

5. **Be the source AFTER Wikipedia** ‚Äî Don't try to out-Wikipedia Wikipedia. Be the next step: deeper analysis, current data, expert opinion it can't provide.

6. **Add citations, statistics, and expert quotes to your content** ‚Äî The Princeton GEO study proves these boost visibility by 22-115%.

7. **Structure content for RAG extraction:**
   - Lead with direct answers (first paragraph answers the query)
   - Use clear H2/H3 heading hierarchy matching likely search queries
   - Optimal paragraph length: 40-60 words
   - Self-contained sections that work as standalone chunks
   - Include verifiable data points

8. **Create comparative listicles** ‚Äî 32.5% of all AI citations come from this format. "Best X for Y", "Top 10 Z", comparison tables.

9. **Allow AI crawlers** in robots.txt:
   ```
   User-agent: OAI-SearchBot
   Allow: /
   ```

10. **Know your citation neighbours** ‚Äî ChatGPT cites competitors side-by-side. Ensure your content accounts for what competitors are saying.

#### Don't Bother With
- llms.txt (zero impact proven)
- Keyword stuffing (negative impact proven)
- Trying to be cited alone ‚Äî ChatGPT cites 4-6 sources per turn

### 5.1 What Makes AI Recommend One Employer Over Another?

Based on the general citation factors above, applied specifically to employer brand content:

#### Factor 1: Brand Search Volume (Critical)
- If job seekers are actively searching for "[Company Name] jobs" or "[Company Name] reviews", this directly increases AI citation probability
- **Actionable:** Invest in employer brand awareness campaigns that drive branded searches
- Companies with strong consumer brands have a built-in advantage
- **Evidence strength:** Proven (0.334 correlation applies to all domains including employer brands)

#### Factor 2: Multi-Platform Presence (Critical)
- Companies mentioned on 4+ platforms are 2.8x more likely to appear
- For employer brands, this means: Company website careers page + Glassdoor + LinkedIn + Indeed + Reddit (r/cscareerquestions, r/jobs, etc.) + Industry publications
- **Gap opportunity:** Most companies have careers pages and Glassdoor profiles but lack Reddit presence and Wikipedia coverage

#### Factor 3: Comparative Listicle Presence (High Impact)
- 32.5% of AI citations come from comparative listicles
- Getting included in "Best Companies to Work For", "Top Employers in [Industry]", "Best Places for Remote Work" type articles is extremely high-value
- These articles serve as the employer brand equivalent of product comparison content
- **Actionable:** Pursue media placements in comparison/ranking articles

#### Factor 4: Glassdoor/Indeed and the Data Gap
- **Critical insight:** Glassdoor and Indeed have **restricted AI crawler access** for much of their content
- This creates a significant **information vacuum** for AI models about employer reputation
- When AI models can't access Glassdoor reviews, they rely more heavily on:
  - The employer's own careers content
  - Reddit discussions about working at the company
  - News articles about company culture
  - LinkedIn company page information
- **Strategic opportunity:** Fill the gap that Glassdoor blocking creates by publishing comprehensive employer information on your own domain

#### Factor 5: Structured Careers Content
- Schema markup matters significantly for AI citation
- Implement `Organization`, `JobPosting`, `EmployerAggregateRating` schema
- Create structured salary data pages with proper `<table>` HTML
- FAQ schema addressing common candidate questions: "What is it like to work at [Company]?", "What is the average salary at [Company]?", "Does [Company] offer remote work?"

### 5.2 How Salary Pages Affect AI Citations

- Salary transparency pages with structured data are highly citable by AI
- AI models answering "What does [Company] pay for [Role]?" will look for:
  - Structured salary range data on the employer's own website
  - Comparison tables (47% higher citation rate)
  - Clear, machine-readable formats
- If your salary data is only on Glassdoor/Indeed (which may be blocked from AI crawlers), the AI may hallucinate or use outdated information
- **Actionable:** Publish salary ranges on your careers domain with proper schema markup

### 5.3 How llms.txt Files Affect Employer Citations

**They don't.** As proven by the Senthor (10M requests, 0 reads) and SE Ranking (300K domains, no correlation) studies, llms.txt has zero measurable impact on AI citations. This applies equally to employer brand content.

However, there is a **different strategic value** for employer brands: creating an `llms.txt`-style structured summary of your employer value proposition could be useful as a **template for future AI integration** if the standard ever gains traction. Low effort, no current benefit, minimal downside.

## Methodology Notes

This research was conducted on 19 February 2026 using:
- Browser-based Google Search for source discovery
- Direct URL fetching of identified articles and studies
- Cross-referencing of claims across multiple independent sources
- Prioritisation of studies with clear methodology documentation and large datasets

### Evidence Classification System Used

| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Proven by study | Peer-reviewed academic paper or controlled experiment with clear methodology |
| ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Proven by study | Large-scale industry study (1,000+ data points) with published methodology |
| ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê Industry consensus | Multiple authoritative sources agree; mechanism is plausible |
| ‚≠ê‚≠ê Correlational | Data shows correlation but causation not established |
| ‚≠ê Unproven | Reasonable inference or anecdotal; limited evidence |

### Limitations

- Most studies focus on English-language, US-centric queries. International patterns may differ.
- AI systems are rapidly evolving. Findings from Q4 2025 may not fully apply by Q3 2026.
- Some sources (Snezzi, Relixir) are from companies selling AI SEO services, creating potential bias toward overstating optimisation impact.
- ChatGPT's citation behaviour varies per conversation ‚Äî no study can capture all patterns.
- Claude-specific research is notably thin compared to ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews.
